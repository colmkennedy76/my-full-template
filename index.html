Welcome


Welcome to the 2 Tone Story.

A website dedicated to the music, style and history of the ska revival and 2 Tone movement of late 1970s/early 1980s Britain.

Explore the heritage of this rich, ecclectic musical culture from 1940s Jamaica to modern Britain.


Originating in Coventry in the West Midlands of England and with roots stretching back to post-World War 2, the British ska revival first emerged in the late 1970s with the formation of The Specials.

The original line up of the band included Jerry Dammers, founder of the influential 2 Tone record label, its black and white motif representing the aspiration towards racial harmony in the context of Thatcher's Britain.

The first release of the fledgeling label - also The Specials' first single - Gangsters, reached number six in the UK music charts paving the way for the emergence of revival ska as a significant genre on the British music scene.

Other British ska revival bands achieving recognition and chart success during the following years included The Selecter, The Beat, Madness and Bad Manners.
